## What Exactly is A2 Milk vs A1 Milk?

To understand why A2 milk is superior, we have to look at the protein structure. Milk contains proteins, specifically casein and whey. Casein makes up about 80% of the total protein in cow's milk, and there are two main types of beta-casein: A1 and A2.

Thousands of years ago, all cows produced milk containing exactly zero A1 protein. Every cow was an A2 cow. However, a genetic mutation occurred in European cattle breeds (like Holsteins, the classic black-and-white cows you see on milk cartons), causing them to produce the A1 beta-casein protein. Because these European cows produce enormous quantities of milk, they became the standard for the modern global dairy industry.

The problem? When the human body breaks down A1 protein, it creates a peptide called BCM-7 (Beta-casomorphin-7). Research has heavily linked BCM-7 to digestive issues, inflammation, and gut discomfort. If you think you're lactose intolerant, there's a shockingly high chance you're actually just reacting to the A1 protein and the BCM-7 it releases.

A2 milk, exclusively sourced from indigenous cows like the Gir breed raised at Amrit Milk, contains only the A2 beta-casein protein. It digests clean, causing none of the inflammatory responses associated with A1 milk.

## The Great Mutation: How All Milk Changed

To understand the problem, you need to understand the history of the cow. Thousands of years ago, all domesticated cows in the world produced milk containing solely the A2 beta-casein protein. This is the original, unmutated protein structure. Indigenous Indian breeds, like the majestic Gir cows raised at Amrit Milk, never lost this original genetic structure.

However, several thousand years ago, a genetic mutation occurred in European cattle breeds (primarily Holsteins, the black-and-white cows that now dominate the global dairy industry). This mutation caused them to start producing a different form of beta-casein known as A1.

Because these European breeds were capable of producing drastically higher volumes of milk, they were mass-bred and exported globally during the industrial revolution. As a result, almost all commercial milk you buy in a standard grocery store today is primarily A1 milk.

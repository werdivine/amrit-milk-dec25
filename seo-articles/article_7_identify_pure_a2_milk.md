## 2. The Homogenization Test: Does the Cream Rise to the Top?

Open a standard carton of generic A2 milk from the supermarket. Does it look perfectly smooth, stark white, and thin? If so, you are drinking homogenized milk, and you should reconsider your purchase immediately.

Homogenization is an unnatural, violent mechanical process where milk is forced through microscopic holes at intense pressure. This shatters the fat molecules, dispersing them permanently throughout the liquid so the cream never separates. Unfortunately, breaking the fat molecules makes the fat highly susceptible to oxidation, altering the structure of the milk and making it slightly inflammatory.
